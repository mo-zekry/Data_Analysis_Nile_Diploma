### âŒ Myth 1: More Data = Better Insights
**âœ… Reality**: Quality > Quantity. Clean, relevant data is more valuable than vast amounts of poor data.

**âœ… Reality**: Requires business understanding, communication, and critical thinking alongside technical skills.

### âŒ Myth 3: Data Never Lies
**âœ… Reality**: Data can be factual, but collection, analysis, and interpretation can introduce bias. Context is crucial.

### âŒ Myth 4: Need Advanced Math
**âœ… Reality**: Basic math is often sufficient; complexity depends on the goal. Statistical knowledge is valuable but not always advanced.

### âŒ Myth 5: Correlation = Causation
**âœ… Reality**: Correlation shows a relationship, not cause-and-effect. Further investigation is needed for causation.

### âŒ Myth 6: Visualization is Just Aesthetics
**âœ… Reality**: Effective visualization is about clear communication of insights, making complexity understandable.

### âŒ Myth 7: Advanced Tools > Simple Tools
**âœ… Reality**: The *right* tool depends on the task. Sometimes Excel is better than complex code.

### âŒ Myth 8: Analysis is a One-Time Project
**âœ… Reality**: It's typically an ongoing process as conditions change and new questions arise.

### âŒ Myth 9: Automation Replaces Analysts
**âœ… Reality**: AI enhances capabilities, but human judgment, domain expertise, and critical thinking remain essential.

### âŒ Myth 10: Analysis Always Gives Clear Answers
